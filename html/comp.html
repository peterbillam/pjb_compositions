Peter Billam's Compositions

  New !   Eight Guitar Pieces, 2017

This is a set of eight pieces for guitar, intended for either steel or nylon strings.
They grew out of what I learned while transcribing the Fingerstyle Guitar Solos.
Included are:  Twinkled, WaveRide, TwoWheel, Glooming, DawnPipe, TalkSoft, BlueMoon and Pluckier.

The score is freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.

  New !   Album for Choir, 2015

This is an album of seven pieces for choir :   Sometimes for three voices, Konkerado for four, Web for four, Only the glories of the night for three (of which the last bass-note divides), Well I Never for five, Remember for four, and The Spell for five (of which the top voice divides).
Konkerado could last anywhere between 60 sec and 150 sec, according to breath and acoustic; the esperanto jx sounds like the french soft j as in je.  

The score is freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.

  New !   Two Encores for Strings, 2015

These are two short pieces,  lasting about 2min 20sec and 2min,  written for two violins, two violas, and two cellos.

They share the 2+2+2 sextet instrumentation of Music for Strings, and of my transcription of Bach's Ricercare a 6, and of Brahms' Sextets Op.18 and Op.36, Rimsky-Korsakov's sextet in A, Dvorjak's Op.48, Schoenberg's chamber version of Verklaerte Nacht, Strauss' Op.85 sextet from Capriccio, Korngold's Op.10 sextet, and are not far from the chamber-septet version of John Adams' Shaker Loops.  So in the event of a concert involving those pieces, it is hoped these Two Encores for Strings might be useful.

The score is freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.
Likewise, the parts are freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.

  New !   Music for Strings, 2015

This piece is in three movements, and lasts about 15 minutes. It is written for six players, two violins, two violas, two cellos, but performances with twelve or more players are also intended.

It shares the 2+2+2 sextet instrumentation of Two Encores for Strings, and of my transcription of Bach's Ricercare a 6, and of Brahms' Sextets Op.18 and Op.36, Rimsky-Korsakov's sextet in A, Dvorjak's Op.48, Schoenberg's chamber version of Verklaerte Nacht, Strauss' Op.85 sextet from Capriccio, and Korngold's Op.10 sextet. It's also not too far from the chamber-septet version of John Adams' Shaker Loops (which adds a bass, and uses three violins and one viola rather than two and two).

The score is freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.
Likewise, the parts are freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.

Twelve Canons, 2010

These are a set of strict canons: the first at one semitone distance, the second at two semitones, the third at three, and so on until the last at twelve semitones. All are now completed !
All may be played on piano, Three also on string trio, Four needs four hands, Five needs three or four hands, and Nine is most practical with three. Ten is good with three hands, or with piano and string quartet or quintet, or with string orchestra (separate parts are not included, so if you're contemplating a performance and would like string parts, let me know), Eleven is good with four hands, or with violin, viola and two celli, or with flute, clarinet and two basoons, or with flute, clarinet and piano, and Twelve needs four hands.

Synthesiser-performances in MP3 form are available of the Canons at One, Two, Three, Four, Five, Six, Seven, Eight, Nine, Ten, Eleven and Twelve semitones.

Check out number Four on YouTube at www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZBLOgRjtEho
and number Eleven at www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cd8pFTK0edg

The score is freely available for A4 printers, or for US-Letter printers.
